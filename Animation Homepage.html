>>>>>>Animation<<<<<< There are many types of animation, including 2D, stop-motion, 3D hand-drawn and computer-generated, but all roles call for high levels of creativity and passion. >>>>>>What is Animation<<<<<< Animation is a filmmaking technique created by animators which still images are manipulated to create moving images. In traditional animation, images are drawn or painted by hand on transparent celluloid sheets (cels) to be photographed and exhibited on film. >>>>>>What does a animator do<<<<<< Using their creativity and modern art skills, animators create a series of images called animations to include in various media formats. Some of their creations incorporate the use of storyboards for movies, avatars for video games and other digital graphics. >>>>>>Work experience<<<<<< Animation is a competitive industry. To find work you must have a showreel. This is your portfolio to show to production companies or animation commissioners in advertising agencies, music companies or television companies. >>>>>>How to become an animator<<<<<< A career in animation begins with a love for artistic expression, especially in a multimedia capacity. Before you pursue your education in this field, consider your overall career goals and take time to research the various options available to you. >>>>>>Job Outlook<<<<<< Employment of special effects artists and animators is projected to grow 5 percent from 2021 to 2031, about as fast as the average for all occupations. About 6,700 openings for special effects artists and animators are projected each year, on average, over the decade. >>>>>><<<<<< If you wish to become an animator if you enjoy being creative and making new things. Animators use creative skills almost daily to form story ideas and develop new ways of drawing or animating stories. In addition, if you're working on an independent animation project, you may have the creative freedom to make it however you wish.
