PROGRAMMING


Computer programming or coding is the composition of sequences of instructions, called programs, that computers can follow to perform tasks. It involves designing and implementing algorithms, step-by-step specifications of procedures, by writing code in one or more programming languages. Programmers typically use high-level programming languages that are more easily intelligible to humans than machine code, which is directly executed by the central processing unit. Proficient programming usually requires expertise in several different subjects, including knowledge of the application domain, details of programming languages and generic code libraries, specialized algorithms, and formal logic.



Programming is, quite literally, all around us. From the take-out we order, to the movies we stream, code enables everyday actions in our lives. Tech companies are no longer recognizable as just software companies — instead, they bring food to our door, help us get a taxi, influence outcomes in presidential elections, or act as a personal trainer.



Programmers often work in teams, and effective communication helps in collaborating with team members. By clearly expressing your ideas and understanding others, you can work together smoothly and achieve project goals. Communication skills are crucial when interacting with clients. Being able to understand their requirements and clarify any doubts ensures that you deliver the desired results. Active listening and asking questions can help in gathering accurate information.
